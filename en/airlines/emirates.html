About Emirates

Emirates was founded in Dubai (UAE) in 1985 and is the flag carrier of the United Arab Emirates. Operating from Dubai International Airport (DXB) as its hub, Emirates serves 150+ cities across 6 continents, making it one of the world's largest international airlines.

The biggest advantage: the UAE has zero personal income tax. A ¥45M salary is 100% take-home pay. Combined with generous housing and education allowances, Emirates is one of the most financially attractive careers for pilots worldwide.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Emirates captain salary in 2026?

Monthly total approximately AED 64,757 (basic AED 42,695 + housing AED 16,075 + flying pay ~AED 6,000). Annualized: ~AED 777,000 (~¥45M). UAE has zero personal income tax, so this is full take-home.

Is Emirates salary really tax-free?

Yes. The UAE has no personal income tax. 100% of gross salary is take-home. US citizens may owe US taxes but benefit from the Foreign Earned Income Exclusion (up to ~$100,800/year).

What are the requirements to join Emirates as a pilot?

Captain: 3,000+ PIC hours, ATPL, ICAO English Level 4+. First Officer: 1,500+ hours, ATPL (frozen) or CPL+IR. Work visa fully sponsored. Emirates plans to hire 1,500+ pilots in 2025–2026.

How does Emirates compare to ANA or JAL?

Emirates captain (¥45M tax-free) vs ANA/JAL captain (¥27M before tax → ~¥17-18M after Japanese income tax). Emirates has 2–2.5× real purchasing power. Add housing + education = total package ~¥55M+.

How long to upgrade from F/O to Captain?

Typically 5–8 years as First Officer. Depends on vacancies and performance. Emirates also actively recruits Direct Entry Captains with existing type ratings on B777 or A380.
